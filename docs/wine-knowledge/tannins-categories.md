# Wine Categories and Tannins

## Wine Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Red** | Made from black grapes with maceration; rich in tannins and anthocyanins |
| **White** | Made from white (or black) grapes without skin contact; fresh and aromatic |
| **Rosé** | Short skin contact; light colour and structure |
| **Sparkling** | Contains CO₂ from secondary fermentation; includes Champagne, Prosecco, Cava |
| **Fortified** | Alcohol added during fermentation; includes Port, Sherry, Marsala |

## What Are Tannins?

Tannins are natural polyphenolic compounds found primarily in **red wines**. They develop during winemaking when grape skins, seeds, and stems are in contact with the juice.

**What you feel:** a dry, grippy sensation in the mouth — the absence of saliva on the sides of your tongue.

**Why it matters:** tannins contribute to a wine's structure, ageing potential, and complexity. High-tannin wines (like Barolo or Cabernet Sauvignon) pair well with fatty, protein-rich foods because tannins bind to proteins and soften.

## Choosing the Right Glass

The right glass enhances aromas and flavours:

| Wine Type | Recommended Glass |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Red wines** | Large bowl, wide opening — allows the wine to breathe |
| **White wines** | Smaller bowl — preserves freshness and aromatics |
| **Sparkling** | Flute or tulip — retains bubbles and concentrates aromas |
| **Fortified** | Small, narrow glass — concentrates aromas in small pours |
